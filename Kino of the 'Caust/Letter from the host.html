Letter from the Host

WWII War Victims Memorial

Dear Kinokers,

The impact of [WWII] is still felt today. Not only did the war leave the world with the end of colonialism, the race of technological achievements, and the formation of the [United Nations] and multiple independent nations, but also tragedies and extreme ideologies during this dark period of human history, that still wander around the world until today.

Therefore, we would love to introduce you to [Kino of the 'Caust], a platform dedicated to preserving the memories of those affected by the war and its consequences, through the lens of cinema, documentaries, and arts. Our mission is to honor the victims and to contribute to the front of truth and equality, fighting against lies and extreme ideologies.

Through careful curation of films and arts from different perspectives – those of the Axis powers and the Allied forces – we hope to provide supplies for this global campaign as "the pens of dead men, women and children". These films and arts serve as storytellers whose characters had to face up to "human growth"-a disgusting excuse for extreme ideologies and their atrocities. Veterans, victims, and historians all have their stories to tell, all have their role to play; in the script written with blood and tears.

Once again, we appreciate your visit and hope you will find something intriguing and thought-provoking in our collection. As you navigate through our collection, we encourage you to approach each film with an open mind and heart, ready to learn from history's mistakes and lessons.

Remember: The dark side of humanity teaches us the bright side of empathy.

As time keeps moving forward, we must strive for peace and equality!

Sincerely,
Lương Minh Dũng (03-12TH2)

[The Archivist]
